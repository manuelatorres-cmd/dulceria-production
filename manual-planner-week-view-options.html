Why Gantt (A) Stages flow visually left-to-right like real production time. When you sequence Pistachio's polishing on Monday, you can see the rest of the row cascade right and verify nothing collides with another batch's mould or your active hours. Drag a single stage chip → reschedule one piece without moving the whole batch. The day-load row at the bottom tells you if any day is overpacked (Fri shows 3h 25m in red).

When B wins If you stand in the workshop in the morning thinking "what am I polishing today?" — swimlanes answer that in one glance. But that question is what /daily should answer. This page is planning, not execution.

When C wins Closest to the current page mental model — least retraining. But it loses the time-flow you get from horizontal rows. Pistachio's 6 stages over 6 days look like 6 unrelated cards, not a sequence.
